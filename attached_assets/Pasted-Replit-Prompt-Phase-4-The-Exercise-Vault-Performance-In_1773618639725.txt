Replit Prompt: Phase 4 – The Exercise Vault & Performance Intelligence
Objective: Build a searchable, filterable "Exercise Vault" that acts as both a library and a personal performance tracker.

1. Library UI & Filtering (Front-end):

Search & Filter Bar: Allow users to filter the 300+ exercise database by:

Muscle Group: (Chest, Back, Quads, etc. - use a dropdown or icon grid).

Equipment: (Barbell, Dumbbell, Machine, Bodyweight).

Goal: (Strength, Hypertrophy, Mobility).

Exercise Cards: Each card displays a thumbnail/icon, the primary muscle worked, and a "Difficulty" tag (1-10).

2. The "Exercise Detail" View (Deep Dive):

Instructional Content: * Embed a YouTube Player (using a URL field from the database).

Written "Step-by-Step" instructions and "Common Mistakes" cards.

Stimulus Map: A visual breakdown of Primary, Secondary, and Tertiary muscle involvement.

Alternative Recommendations: A "Similar Exercises" section at the bottom that suggests movements with the same stimulus (e.g., if viewing "Back Squat," suggest "Leg Press" or "Goblet Squat").

3. AI Performance Insights (The Intelligence):

Historical Data: Fetch the user's last 3 sessions for this specific exercise (Weight/Reps/Sets).

Predictive Coaching: * Display a "Coach’s Note" card: "Based on your 5x5 at 250lb, your estimated 1-rep max is [X]."

Rest Recommendation: "To hit your strength goal, rest 3 mins between sets; for growth, 60-90s."

Plateau Alert: If progress has stalled for 3 sessions, surface a tip: "You've plateaued here. Try swapping to [Alternative Exercise] for 2 weeks to change the stimulus."

4. Back-end Requirements:

Database Integration: Connect to the ExerciseLibrary table (300+ entries) and the WorkoutHistory table.

Relational Mapping: Ensure each exercise has an alternative_ids field to link similar movements.

Analytics Logic: Create a helper function to calculate "Estimated 1RM" (One Rep Max) and "Total Volume" (Weight × Reps × Sets) per exercise.

What we included from our brainstorm:
The "Why": Connecting the exercise back to their specific goal.

The "How": YouTube embeds and "What to watch out for" cards.

The Variety: The stack-ranked alternatives to keep them excited and account for busy gyms.

The Coach: AI-driven predictions and rest-time suggestions.
